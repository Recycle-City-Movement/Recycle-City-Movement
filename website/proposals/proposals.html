Proposals

Any member can make a proposal. The member fills out and submits a proposal from the choices below so that it may be voted upon by the voter pool. The voter pool is dynamic in that it is created from the RCM active membership ranks. There are different kinds of proposals that members can create:


  • a new community;
  • support an existing community;
  • a miscellaneous charity fund donation;
  • a change to a business document or process.

Proposals go through phases:

  1. During the first week a proposal is classified as a new proposal.

  2. After seven days, the proposal is judged by the number of votes it has received.

  3. If the proposal receives votes of at least 33% of its voter pool, it becomes active.

  4. At this stage, if the proposal is not active, it is deleted from the ballot box and archived. The proposal is dead.

  5. The active proposal continues to remain in the ballot box for an additional week, receiving votes.

  6. At the end of the two week period, the proposal is judged for its value. If the proposal has received votes from more than 50% of its voter pool, then it is viewed as valued. Otherwise, the proposal is dead.

  7. If the valued proposal received more than 50% of the votes in its favor, then the proposal is adopted, and its implementation process begins. Otherwise, the proposal is dead.

  8. During the implementation of the valued proposal, the admins work with each other to sort out what changes need to take place, and what actions need to occur. Also a progress report is created and maintained, that is visible from the RCM website, detailing the steps taken to implement the proposal.

  9. If the proposal is a community funding proposal, then the Community Contract is involved. Thus the would-be Community Founder, the initiator, is notified of the proposal's outcome, and given a detailed documentation on what steps need to be completed for the funding to finalize.

    Most importantly, the initiator and an additional two community representatives sign and notarize the Community Contract and submit the contract to a designated RCM finance administrator. RCM will then review the contract and sign-off on it by having two finance admins and one addition administrator sign the contract. This is the final phase of community funding proposal and lasts as long as ninety days. If a contract is not signed by RCM admin and the community representatives in that period of time, the proposal dies.

    If a completed contract is done on time, then it archived on the RCM website. If it is a new community funding, then a fund distribution transfer is completed by the coordination of two RCM finance admins.
