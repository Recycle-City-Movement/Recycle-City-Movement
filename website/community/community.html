Community

The community page is for development and connecting communities.

Development

RCM funds the development of new communities and the transformation of existing communities.

If you'd like to become a Community Founder, one who purchases land and other assets for establishing a community, or if you'd like to establish funding for your existing community, then first read the community charter and community business plan .

RCM has a few requirements for supporting communities:

The first step is to get funding approved. This is done by submitting a proposal for a new community or an existing community.

If the proposal is accepted by the voting pool. RCM will ask that the community founder to complete two incorporation processes. The community needs two incorporate two legal entities:

  • Create a non-profit organization that donates recycled products to the greater population. The community produces the products, and takes donations to the non-profit's charity fund that it owns and promotes on a community website. RCM has a community website template that communities can use to facilitate the website creation process.
  • Incorporate a for-profit business that funds a unity fund. This fund pays community expenses and its members. Most of the business model is up to community. This is an opportunity for a community to sustain itself by producing goods and/or services from a functioning business.

The last step to becoming a supported RCM community is that three community members, and the three RCM finance administrators, need to sign a RCM community contract. The contract is composed of a community charter and a community business plan.
